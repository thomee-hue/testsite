The History of the Masters

The Masters Tournament was founded in 1934 by golfer Bobby Jones and Clifford Roberts. It is heald annually at Augusta National Golf Club and is one of the four major championships in pro golf.

Bobby Jones designed Augusta National with the help of renowned golf course architect Alister MacKenzie. The course opened in 1932, and the first Masters Tournament was played in 1934. Horton Smith won that event with a score of 284. The tournament was renamed "The Masters" in 1939, a name that Jones had initially resisted but eventually embraced.

Masters fun facts

  • Founded: 1934
  • Most Wins: Jack Nicklaus (6 titles)
  • Par 72 (7,555 yards)

Traditions and Iconic Moments

The Masters is famouse for its rich traditions. Every year the tournament begins with an honorary starter ceremony where legends of the game hit the first tee shot. Winners become members of Augusta National and can wear their green jacket at the club.

Over the decades, the Masters has produced some of the most memorable moments in golf history. Tiger Woods' dominant 1997 victory where he won by a record 12 strokes, is widely considered one of the greatest performances in golf history.
